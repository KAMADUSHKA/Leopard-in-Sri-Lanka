Sigiriya – A Fortress Carved in Stone and Legend

Rising dramatically from the lush central plains of Sri Lanka, Sigiriya, also known as Lion Rock, is one of the island’s most awe-inspiring historical landmarks. This UNESCO World Heritage Site, once the royal citadel of King Kasyapa in the 5th century, is a masterpiece of ancient urban planning, architecture, and engineering.

Best Time to Visit

"Sigiriya can be visited year-round, but the ideal time is during the dry season from December to April, when skies are clear and the climb is more enjoyable. Early mornings or late afternoons are best to avoid the midday heat and enjoy panoramic views with fewer crowds."

TA Journey Through History and Legend

The rock fortress of Sigiriya is more than just a climb—it’s a journey into the heart of Sri Lanka’s rich past. Towering nearly 200 meters above the surrounding jungle, this colossal rock is crowned with the ruins of an ancient palace and surrounded by elaborate gardens, reservoirs, and frescoes.

As you ascend the rock, you'll pass the famed Mirror Wall, admire the stunning Sigiriya Frescoes, and encounter the massive Lion’s Paws—remnants of a once-majestic lion-shaped gateway that gave the fortress its name. At the summit, you’re rewarded with sweeping views and the remains of the royal palace.

Why Visit Pinnawala?

🟢 Ancient Royal Citadel – Explore the remains of a 5th-century king’s palace perched atop a monolithic rock.

🟢 Architectural Marvel – Marvel at sophisticated water gardens, symmetrical layouts, and advanced hydraulic systems.

🟢 Cultural Significance – Learn the story of King Kasyapa, his vision, and the legends that surround this ancient site.

🟢 Incredible Vistas – Enjoy breathtaking panoramic views over lush forests and distant hills.


🟢 UNESCO World Heritage Site – Recognized globally for its historical, cultural, and architectural importance.


A visit to Sigiriya isn’t just about seeing a rock fortress—it’s about stepping into the footsteps of kings, walking through an ancient city in the sky, and experiencing a wonder that has captivated visitors for centuries.
